## SECTION 5: CSA CYBERSECURITY APPRENTICESHIP PROGRAM

### Q: What is the CSA program?
A: CSA stands for Indiana Career Scholarship Account. The CSA Cybersecurity Apprenticeship is a state-funded program that provides free cybersecurity training and certification to eligible Indiana high school students.

### Q: Is the CSA program free?
A: Yes, the CSA program is completely free for eligible students. The state of Indiana provides grants of up to $5,000 per student per school year to cover training costs.

### Q: Who is eligible for the CSA program?
A: The CSA program is available to Indiana high school sophomores, juniors, and seniors. Spots are limited and offered first-come, first-served for each school year.

### Q: What does the CSA program include?
A: The CSA program includes industry-recognized certifications and credentials in IT and Cybersecurity, hands-on practical skills training, U.S. Department of Labor registered apprenticeship opportunities, and optional video game development pathways.

### Q: How do I apply for the CSA program?
A: Visit hopetrainingacademy.org/csa and click Get Started to complete the application. You can also call 317-349-HOPE or 833-209-HOPE. A Success Coach will follow up after you submit your application.

### Q: What certifications can I earn through CSA?
A: Through the CSA program, you can earn CompTIA certifications and other industry-recognized credentials in cybersecurity that employers actively seek.

### Q: Is there a deadline for CSA applications?
A: Scholarships are offered first-come, first-served each year. There are limited slots available, so it's best to apply early to secure your spot.

### Q: Can I do video game development through CSA?
A: Yes, the CSA program offers an optional video game development pathway using the Unity platform, in addition to the core cybersecurity training.

### Q: What careers can CSA lead to?
A: CSA prepares students for careers in cybersecurity, IT support, network administration, security analysis, and related technology fields. Graduates are prepared for entry-level positions and further education.

### Q: Is CSA a real apprenticeship?
A: Yes, the CSA program is a U.S. Department of Labor registered apprenticeship program, meaning it meets federal standards for quality apprenticeship training.
